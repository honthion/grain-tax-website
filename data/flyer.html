A MEMORY PROJECT

HE FED THE NATION.

WHO FEEDS HIM NOW?

He woke up before sunrise, year after year.

Working the same fields, under the same sky.

Every harvest, a portion of his grain was taken — not by choice, but as a duty.

It was called “contribution.”

Now he is old.

His hands are still rough, but the fields are no longer his.

His monthly pension is about $30.

He fed the nation.

But who feeds him now?
